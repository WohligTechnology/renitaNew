Smash grafting

Smashed skin grafting is a modification of ultrathin skin grafting. The shaved off graft is smashed into smaller pieces and applied over dermabraded donor area. Procedure is simple and quick. The graft size taken is also smaller when compared with ultrathin skin grafting.

Hair follicular grafting

Follicular units are extracted from the occiput and implanted in depigmented skin involving hairy areas of the body. The pigment match is after repigmentation is good. As the pigment spread is limited from each follicular unit, the grafts have to be closely placed and the procedure is not applicable to non-hairy skin.

TISSUE GRAFTING

Miniature punch grafting

Small punches of skin from retro-auricular or thigh is taken and grafted on recipient area of depigmented skin. Smaller punches are used to avoid cobble stoning. The procedure is quite simple and requires relatively lesser time.

Suction blister grafting

Blisters are created using negative pressure with syringes. Blister roofs are cut from the donor area and grafted to the recipient site prepared using dermabrasion. Advantages include uniform appearance without cobble stoning but the procedure is time consuming and cannot be done for larger areas.
